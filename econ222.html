Experimental Economics is a field of economics where we collect data and test hypotheses about human behavior in a controlled setting.

The subjects in economics experiments are college students, i.e. YOU!

Subjects earn money for participating in the experiment and the amount earned depends on their behavior in the experiment: better performance in the experiment leads to more money.

Everyone who participates and completes the experiment will earn a $10 show-up fee.

Everyone who participates will earn additional money. The amount will depend on their performance in the experiment.

Likely earnings are between $20 and $40 (although could be more or less).

At the beginning of the experiment, we will explain exactly how you earn money during the experiment.

The experiment will take approximately 90 minutes.
